### Question 6C-E3 (multi-part)

**Date added:** 2026-06-18
**Chapter/Section:** 6C, 6G
**Concept:** Angle of elevation + cosine rule combined
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Source trigger:** Exam prep
**Source:** Canberra Grammar School, 2024 Yearly (Q19)

#### Question
A vertical gate post TF stands at point F. From point G, the angle of elevation of the top of the post (T) is 30°, and GF = 3.5 m. From point L, the angle of elevation of T is 40°. The angle GFL = 120°.

(a) Find the height of the post TF. Give your answer to the nearest metre.
(b) Find the distance LF. Give your answer to the nearest metre.
(c) Find the distance GL using the cosine rule. Give your answer to the nearest metre.

#### Expected Method
(a) tan 30° = h/3.5 → h = 3.5 × tan 30° ≈ 2 m
(b) tan 40° = h/LF → LF = h/tan 40° ≈ 2/0.839 ≈ 2.4 m (rounds to 2 m)
(c) GL² = GF² + LF² − 2 × GF × LF × cos(120°)
Use unrounded values for best accuracy.

#### Answer
(a) h ≈ 2 m
(b) LF ≈ 2 m
(c) GL ≈ 5 m

**Tutor note:** Part (c) requires carrying through accurate (unrounded) values from (a) and (b). The final answer of 5 m is correct regardless of minor rounding differences.

#### Common Trap
Rounding intermediate answers too early; using vertical height instead of slant in the wrong formula.

#### Question
In triangle ABC, angle A = 18°, angle B = 127°, and BC = 25.8 cm. Find the length AC (marked x) to 1 decimal place.

#### Expected Method
Angle C = 180° − 18° − 127° = 35°.
Side x = AC is opposite angle B (127°); side BC = 25.8 is opposite angle A (18°).

Wait — side x is AC, which is opposite angle B. Side BC = 25.8 is opposite angle A. But that gives a very large x. Re-check: BC is opposite A, AC is opposite B.

x/sin 127° = 25.8/sin 18°
x = 25.8 × sin 127°/sin 18°

#### Answer
x ≈ 66.7 cm

#### Common Trap
Matching the wrong side to the wrong angle. Always identify which side is opposite which angle before writing the sine rule fraction.

### Question 6G-E5 (multi-part)

**Date added:** 2026-06-18
**Chapter/Section:** 6D, 6G
**Concept:** Cosine rule — finding an angle from three sides; then angle of elevation
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Source trigger:** Exam prep
**Source:** The Scots College, 2024 Assessment Task 4 (Q21a)

#### Question
A hot air balloon is 300 m from point A and 500 m from point B. Points A and B are 600 m apart on the ground.

(a) Find the angle at B (the angle ABalloon) using the cosine rule. Round to the nearest degree.
(b) Using your answer to (a), find the height of the balloon above the ground. Round to the nearest metre.

#### Expected Method
(a) The side opposite the angle at B is AB-to-balloon = 300 m. The two sides forming the angle at B are 500 m (B to balloon) and 600 m (B to A).

cos θ = (500² + 600² − 300²) / (2 × 500 × 600) = 520000/600000 ≈ 0.867
θ = cos⁻¹(0.867) ≈ 30°

(b) sin 30° = h/500 → h = 500 × 0.5 = 250 m

#### Answer
(a) θ = 30°
(b) h = 250 m

#### Common Trap
(a) Putting the wrong side as the "opposite" — the opposite side is the one not touching the angle you're finding.
(b) Using the horizontal distance (600 m) instead of the slant distance (500 m) as the hypotenuse.

#### Mastery Link

**Source trigger:** Exam prep
**Source:** The Scots College, 2024 Assessment Task 4 (Q22)

#### Question
Earlsfield (E) is on a bearing of 085° from Croydon (C) and on a bearing of 155° from Dorking (D). Dorking is on a bearing of 040° from Croydon. The distance between Dorking and Earlsfield is 68 km.

Find the distance of Earlsfield from Croydon, to the nearest metre.

#### Expected Method
Find interior angles of triangle CDE:
- Angle at C (between CD and CE): 085° − 040° = 45°
- Back-bearing from D to C is 040° + 180° = 220°. Angle at D (between DC and DE): 220° − 155° = 65°
- Angle at E: 180° − 45° − 65° = 70°

Sine rule (DE is opposite angle C = 45°; CE is opposite angle D = 65°):
CE/sin 65° = 68/sin 45°
CE = 68 × sin 65°/sin 45° ≈ 87.156 km

#### Answer
CE ≈ 87,156 m (or 87.156 km)

#### Common Trap
Getting the interior angles wrong — the hardest part is converting compass bearings to angles inside the triangle. Sketch north lines at each point before calculating.

#### Mastery Link
Bearings → interior angles → sine rule

### Question 6DF-E2 (multi-part)

**Date added:** 2026-06-18
**Chapter/Section:** 6D, 6F
**Concept:** Bearings problem with sine rule; back-bearing
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Source trigger:** Exam prep
**Source:** Canberra Grammar School, 2024 Yearly (Q18)

#### Question
A, C, and D are on a north-south line, with C 175 km north of A. The bearing of E from A is 055° and the bearing of E from C is 134°.

(a) Find the bearing of A from E.
(b) Find CE to the nearest km.

#### Expected Method
(a) Back-bearing of A from E: 055° + 180° = 235°T

(b) Interior angles of triangle ACE:
- Angle at A = 55° (bearing gives angle from north)
- Bearing of E from C is 134° → interior angle at C = 180° − 134° = 46°
- Angle at E = 180° − 55° − 46° = 79°

Sine rule (AC = 175 km is opposite angle E = 79°; CE is opposite angle A = 55°):
CE/sin 55° = 175/sin 79°
CE = 175 × sin 55°/sin 79° ≈ 146 km

#### Answer
(a) 235°T
(b) CE ≈ 146 km

#### Common Trap
(a) Forgetting to add 180° for the back-bearing.
(b) Misidentifying which angle is opposite which side — draw and label the triangle fully before applying the sine rule.

#### Mastery Link
Back-bearings; bearings → triangle angles → sine rule

## 6D & 6G: Bearings Combined with Cosine Rule — Exam Questions

### Question 6DG-E1 (multi-part)

**Date added:** 2026-06-18
**Chapter/Section:** 6D, 6G
**Concept:** Bearings with cosine rule
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Source trigger:** Exam prep
**Source:** NBCS, 2023 Yearly (Short Answer Q29)

#### Question
R is on a bearing of 030°T and a distance of 50 km from Q. R is due east of P. S is 45 km due east of R. The angle GFL (or here: angle between QR and RS at point R) = 120°.

(a) Find the distance PS.
(b) Show that angle QRS = 120°.
(c) Find the distance QS to 1 decimal place.

#### Expected Method
(a) In triangle PQR (right angle at R since R is due east of P):
PR = QR × sin 30° = 50 × 0.5 = 25 km
PS = PR + RS = 25 + 45 = 70 km

(b) QR makes 30° with the north (vertical). RS is due east (horizontal, 90° from north). Exterior angle = 90° + 30° = 120°. ∴ ∠QRS = 120°.

(c) Cosine rule in triangle QRS:
QS² = 50² + 45² − 2(50)(45) cos 120°
= 2500 + 2025 − 4500 × (−0.5)
= 4525 + 2250 = 6775
QS = √6775 ≈ 82.3 km

#### Answer
(a) 70 km
(b) Shown above
(c) QS ≈ 82.3 km

#### Common Trap
(b) Students often confuse interior and exterior angles when working from bearings. A sketch with a north line at R makes this clear.
(c) cos 120° = −0.5, so the subtraction becomes addition. Missing this sign flip is the most common error.

#### Mastery Link
Bearings → exterior angles → cosine rule with obtuse angle
